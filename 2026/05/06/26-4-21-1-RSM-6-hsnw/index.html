【RSM】 Chapter 6 Hello Snowie

发表于2026-05-06|更新于2026-05-06|艺术文学拙作Reminiscences de Snowflake Mapleleaf
|浏览量:

The small Kirin City lies in the sloping valley of northeastern Westplain, in the middle runs a quiet river called Stream of Silence. When the moon shone at where it always was, the stars would come together and performed a round dance in the night sky. It was not until this time would those clouds lazily open the curtain.

Kirin’s lights went off one by one, which made the city more sleepy than while ago. The roads were totally empty, with only streetlamps awoke in the breezing wind. This was how Kirin City looked liked eight years ago in Snowflake’s memory.

That night, at least for Snowflake, not all houses kept their eyes shut. One house’s window was still bright. That house, along with the streetlamp beside it, open up a yard of light in the middle of darkness.

The door slowly opened. There was a small kirin crouching on the door frame. Her hair was in color of passionate red and her body was coated with pure but smooth white fur. She stared desparately into the inside, where came out a deep and resonant voice.

“Out.”

The small kirin crouched even tighter. She buried her face into her hooves.

“OUT!”

The voice became five times louder than before. The small kirin had no choice but to back off.

The position on the door frame was retaken by a male kirin. He looked down at the small kirin who was already outside, but kept going. Each time he forwarded for one step, she backed off for two, until he thought she would never have the chance to dashed back to the house.

“Please…”

PONG!

The door was slammed shut. The small kirin trembled on the damp ground.

A while later, a black kirin shadow passed through her, with powerful footsteps and unstoppable speed. That kirin from the darkness pounded fiercely on the door.

“Who are you? What do you want?” That male kirin again, answered the door.

“It doesn’t matter who I am.”

“Why?”

“Because you had just abandoned your daughter outside!”

The male kirin burst out a laugh, “So it is my fault then?” He point at the small kirin, “Four hours had passed, she refused to write a single stroke on her homework. I allowed her to skip those hard questions, but not for those simple ones? That is her problem of attitude!”

“Have you ever thought why she didn’t move for a single word? She had always listened to you, but why not today? Were her happiness, her life, even cheaper than a simple question? If there was someone has evil intentions, you will lost your only child!”

The male kirin was silent, but he didn’t gave up a single inch.

“Oh, sorry, that doesn’t matter either.” The mysterious kirin suddenly left out a cold smile, “You don’t want her anyway. I just wonder whether your Morningcloud would agree.”

“Who are you! How do you know my wife’s name! Why did you looked so similar to my daughter!”

“What? Can’t I?”

“Get out of the way. She needs to go home.”

“Go home?” The stranger held door tightly, “Too late!”

The door was slammed shut, again.

The little kirin was still trembling under the streetlamp. The stranger walked towards her and gave her a hug, “It’s okay, dear, I’m here, nobody could hurt you.”

“Who… who are you? Why did you help me?”

“Isn’t it obvoius…” The stranger reached out her right hoof.

When Snowflake was 5, a small area of skin on her right hoof was injured by a burning coal. and that area had never grown the fur back since then.

“… Snowie?”

“It’s just so COOL!”

Two kirins were on the pavement along the river. Snowflake walked steadily on the left, while Snowie was bouncing up and down. “That’s it! I won’t go back! You’ll guard me and guide me, forever!”

Snowflake blushed. She sat down on a bench and pulled Snowie to sit beside her. “What about Mama and Papa?”

“None of my business.” Snowie pouted, “They abandoned me first! Who cares? But now I have a kirin who knows me best in the world!”

“No, no, not like that.” Snowflake shook her head. She watched Snowie in the eye. “They won’t truly abandon you, dear. They just… lost control.”

“Just ‘lost control’? You said so, they set me in danger! Who knows what they will do next time!”

“Snowie, listen.” Snowflake put her hoof on her shoulder, “You know Mama and Papa, right? Are they scary devils that you should risk your life to escape from them?”

Snowie’s moved her sight away.

“My dear,” Snowflake continued, “everyone could lost control.. It won’t last long but, still could turn loving ones into distant enemies. So don’t make silly decisions that you’ll regret.”

“But… Papa really hurt me so bad…” Snowie looked down.

“Then forgive him.” Snowflake insisted, “You actually know that from the bottom of your heart. You can’t lose Papa, just like Papa can’t lose you.”

“You were definitely NOT thinking about these while talking back to Papa.”

“Well that’s another thing.” Snowflake took back her hooves, “He frightened us, then we frightened him back, that’s a draw. But seriously, don’t copy me.”

“Hehe,” Snowie giggled, “so you just… returned to peace again?”

“In fact, Mama let me home half an hour later. And the next day ended like nothing happened.”

“Nothing… happend?” Snowie leaned her head on Snowflake’s shoulder, stretched her front legs and rubbing Snowflake’s hoof. Her legs were exposed under the streetlamp, showing out the oozing red under her white fur.

Snowflake bit her lips, “Yes.”

A gentle breeze slid through their hair strands.

“Hey, what are we doing?” Snowie’s eyes suddenly lit up, “Why’re we wasting time talking about me? I need to know EVERYTHING about you!”

“Huh?”

“Well I had never planned any questions for the future me,” Snowie said while shaking back and forth, “however, you looked much maturer than me.”

“So?”

“I’ve always dreamed about university days. But they’re daily routines for you.”

“Life isn’t just about university, Snowie, I have already started working.”

“Ahhh!” Snowie screamed, “things just get even BETTER! What’s your job, tell me tell me tell me!”

“Um, I think you shouldn’t know.”

“Tell— me—!” Snowie started crawling around in Snowflake’s arms.

Snowflake hate herself. The last word she would say, threw out so natually by herself. Snowflake just can’t do it, Snowie was staring at her, her eyes were as clear as crystal. Hiding something from her would be an impossible challenge.

Snowflake turned her face to the side, leaving a smile on her lips.

“Woo… I know what it means,” Snowie said cunningly, “they don’t let you tell, eh? Only Papa’s job would have that kind of rule.”

Snowflake relieved a deep breath, she nodded.

“Nice.” Snowie grinned. She came closer, kissed Snowflake on her cheek.

“How many stars are there in the sky?”

“Only Luna could know.”

Under the large cape of the night, two kirins were lying on the grass, watching the stars spinning and shining.

“How couldn’t you know?” Snowie asked, “You’re an engineer, like Papa, after all.”

“If you could manage Papa’s wires and instruments to count those stars, that’s fine by me.”

“I could wait,” Snowie looked at Snowflake, “until I’m as old as you.”

Snowflake didn’t reply. Those stars formed into lines and dots, like notes on the blackboard.

“What does an engineer do everyday?”

“Simple. Connect wires, adjust instruments, and read the data.”

“Boring?”

“Not boring.”

“Liar.” Snowie continued looking at the sky, “You named only three things.”

Snowflake grinned. The 14-year-old herself sounds much cleverer than she thought.

“Well, that does sound… repetitive.” Snowflake answered, “But they are certain… You know, those instruments would tell you what it is. You wouldn’t get wrong.”

“Exactly!” Snowie cheered, “That is just what I want. Those instruments do those nasty things for me, without any mistakes. I don’t have to worry what others think, I don’t need to worry what would I fail. I just tell them what to do and they’ll get it done. Right?”

“Right.”

“What about your colleagues?” Snowie asked, “Are they nice too?”

“Fine. Doing each own things, not very loud.”

“That’s perfect. Papa is satisfied with you, no one waits to laugh at you, and even don’t need to stand in front of crowds.”

Snowflake’s breath interrupted for a beat. “Yeah, no need.”

“Well, have you ever thought of not being an…”

“No.”

Snowflake answered in advance, like a musician who came in a beat too early.

Snowie didn’t seemed to notice that. “Hmm, I thought you would consider a job about music.”

Words jammed in Snowflake’s throat. She wanted to speak but spat out nothing.

“Music…” Snowie continued herself, “I love it, really. But in the end, listening is all it’s good for.”

She lifted up her hooves, then put them down.

“If I have to make a living with music, I don’t know what to do.”

Wind blew by the grass, then their fur, then their hair strands. It’s getting a little bit cold, Snowflake thought, were they gonna staying outside for the whole night?

“Hey!” Snowie somehow found her spirit again, “Papa told me they’re assembling a machine. It’s the prototype of ‘Magic Generator’. Is it big?”

“Yeah. Bigger than a house.”

“Really?” Snowie’s eyes lit up like candles, “Was it messy to connect all the wires? I once caught a glimpse of it. Boy, those wires were as many as my hair.”

“Fine, actually. The have colors. Red goes to red and blue goes to blue.”

“What if you made one wrong?”

“We replug it.”

“That’s all?”

“That’s all.”

“Fantastic.” Snowie sighed, “It’s finally over. No self-criticism, no abandonment… Just ‘replug it’.”

Another breeze of wind came. Different from the last one, Snowflake felt her body was getting lighter. Something was flowing away from her heart.

“Where do you eat?”

“Canteen.”

“Woo, great.” Snowie said, “Have you made a very very close friend? You know, being on and off duty together, eat and rest together?”

I could use Autumn Blaze.

“Yes.”

“Is he or she an engineer to?”

No, she’s definitely not an engineer. “She’s… from the department next door.”

“What would you talk about?”

“Work.”

“Just work?”

“Approximately yes.”

“So… Haven’t you ever thought… go for a concert or something?”

“No.”

“Why?”

“No time.”

“Wow, sounds busy.”

“Yeah. Tired everyday. Sometimes I’m too tired to…” Snowflake felt her tonge twisted, “… do anything.”

“Still cool.” Snowie replied.

“Where do you live?”

“An apartment, the organization allocated to us.”

“Roomates?”

“Only me.”

“Congrats!” Snowie clapped, “Now you could play the piano whenever you want.”

“No.”

“Why?”

Snowie would play the piano until her mother calls her for supper.

“No space.”

Snowie frowned.

“What’s the matter? You don’t seemed happy about that.”

“What? No. I’m just tired.”

“You just said being an engineer is stable, but you seemed much more tired than me.”

“Well… looking at instruments is sometimes confusing too.”

Streetlamps along the path they had been started blinking. The first one went out.

The night is getting colder, Snowflake’s breath trembled a little.

“Are you all right?” Snowie sat up.

“I’m fine.” Snowflake answered, “Just the wind.”

The second lamp went off, then the third one.

“H… Hey, why those lamps went off?” Snowie stared tightly on them. “Is that normal?”

“Who knows.” Snowflake reluctantly smiled.

Fourth, fifth. Darkness started encircling them.

“Hey, you promised.” Snowie picked up Snowflake’s hoof, “You’re here, nobody could hurt me.”

“I promise.”

Sixth.

Now the only light was from the moon. Fortunately the moon was shining bright.

“Fine… Where did we stopped?”

“Apartment.”

“Yes, you said you can’t put a piano into the apartment.” Snowie lied close to Snowflake. “So… what did you do on the weekends?”

“Work.”

“Work? All the time?”

“Not… all the time. I sometimes… go to build instruments.”

“What instruments?”

“Magic generator, of course.” Snowflake suddenly caught the suspicious look from Snowie’s eyes. “I mean… we… improved it! Yes. We reduced the size.”

“Hmm… Then what do you do? Still connecting the wires?”

“No… I watch the others do.”

“Watch?”

“Yes. Watch, and check.”

“Check what?”

“Whether it’s right, of course.” Snowflake’s hooves started tapping together.

“But… you’ve got instruments. Shouldn’t they tell you whether it’s right?”

“Something… instruments can’t see.” Snowflake adjusted her posture. “Like… the posture, yes. If you connect those wires with wrong posture, that’s… not right either. I have to stand by their side, to correct them.”

“How to correct?”

“Just… tell them.”

“What if they didn’t listen?”

“Keep telling them.”

“What if they get annoyed?”

“Let them stand at the back of… No, no, just forget it.” Snowflake shook her head.

“Forget it? Then what did you correct?”

Moon hid behind the clouds.

“What are you talking about?” Snowie stood up, her patience was used up. “Engineers don’t care about the posture, Papa had never said about that.”

“I… I just…”

“You stand beside your collegues, but forget about the posture mistakes you said. Do you think they will disappear by themselves?”

“…”

“How do you use you instruments anyway?”

“…”

“You said you read their data, for what? Just tell me what do you do to the instruments!”

“… Play…”

Snowflake’s brain was wiped blank. The word just slipped from her mouth.

“Engineers check the instruments, adjust the instruments, they never play it. Papa had never said about that!”

The wind stopped.

“You said you had never considered a job about music, but you play the instrument at work. Then what kind of job would do that?”

Snowflake could hardly breath.

“Music teacher.”

“What?”

“I’m actually a music teacher.”

“You… what?”

“I’m a music teacher, in Ponyville. I lied to you.”
