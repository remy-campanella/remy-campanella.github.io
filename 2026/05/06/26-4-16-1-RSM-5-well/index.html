【RSM】 Chapter 5 All Is Well

发表于2026-05-06|更新于2026-05-28|艺术文学拙作Reminiscences de Snowflake Mapleleaf
|浏览量:

“Good afternoon, class.”

“Good~ afternoon~ Miss~ Snowflake~.”

“So, let’s continue our new lecture, shall we?” Snowflake walked around the classroom, as the students hurriedly changed their textbook.

“Today’s class is about a magical tool called ‘The Circle of Fifth’.” Snowflake returned to the stage. “Turn to page…” She opened her notebook.

Oh dear.

Her notes stopped at the perfect fifth. Where’s the next one? Snowflake looked over and over, those blank pages contained neither the extensions, nor the textbook knowledge, nor even the titles. Snowflake pressed her hoof on the page, then moved it away, as if her notes would magically appear after that.

“Miss… Snowflake?”

Snowflake raised her head, quickly glanced at the table of the front row. Sweetie Belle’s book was open, and it was turned to the right part.

“… Chapter 13.”

There came lasting sounds of paper rubs. Snowflake picked up a chalk and wrote on the blackboard.


Chapter 13 The Circle of Fifth

Then what?

Snowflake drew a big circle, “Outer, major.” Then a smaller one in the middle, “Inner, minor.” On the top of the outer circle, she wrote a “C”, she nodded. The next one should keep a perfect fifth from C, so “G”. the character was a little bit ugly, she erased it and drew a new one. Then “D”, the chalk broke, the piece fell on the metal frame, making a clear and crisp sound. She slightly stomped on her back hoof, then quickly drew the remained. None of them was neat.

“The circle of fifth displays the relationship between 24 major and minor modes. For each tone on the circle, the right side is its dominant, and the left side is its subdominant.”

Some students turned to some chapters backwards, wondering they had turned to the wrong chapter.

“Miss Snowflake, why do we need the Circle of Fifth?”

“Because… um… with the interval of perfect fifth, no matter which… direction you go, the tone will always… come back. Just remember it, anyway.”

The absence of preparation is killing her, as she needed to pause for at least 10 seconds to formulate a statement. Yet the class still went on, even half of the students had given up keeping up. Their gazes had drifted to the balcony, the outside trees, or simply a crack on the desk.

Snowflake wasn’t in a better condition either. Her knowledge had been concentrated into key words, but her mind jammed while reforming then into fluent sentences. Her eyes fixed on a corner of the blackboard. Those transcendental jargons ran out from her mouth one by one, even herself doubted she could understand her own words.

She hope the class to end.

UGHHHHHHHHH!!!

A large, terrible sound of snoring broke the awkward atmosphere of the class. Snowflake’s writing stopped halfway, the whole class also looked at that corner.

Her student, Diamond Tiara, was lying face down on the table, sleeping soundly.

That poor little chalk in Snowflake’s hoof was smashed into pieces.

“Diamond TiARA!”

“Ah~ Yes, Miss Snowflake?” Diamond Tiara awoke, stretch herself exaggeratedly, and rested her little head on her hooves.

“Are you even a student?” Snowflake scolded, “Stand UP!”

“Whoa, whoa, whoa,” Diamond Tiara laughed, “do you think I’m the only one who want to escape from your stupid class?”

“I DEMAND!” Snowflake was trembling, her face burnt as red as a page of maple leaf, “Stand up, at the back of this room, NOW!”

Diamond Tiara groaned, she walked while twisting her body, then leaned against the back wall.

Snowflake closed her eyes for a while, took a deep breath, then cleared those broken chalks.

The bell rang.

Snowflake slightly waved her hoof, allowing her students to leave.

“Snowflake, wait!”

Snowflake was about to pass under the sign of the school. The warm autumn sunlight spread over her body. When she heard Cheerilee’s voice, she turned around.

Just turning around was not enough, Cheerilee made a guiding gesture to let Snowflake get closer.

“Mrs. Rich wants to see you,” Cheerilee said, “and… she’s not quite happy.”

“Where?”

“In the office.”

The office door was half-opened. Snowflake bit her lips, entered the office slowly and quietly.

A middle-aged mare, colored in pink and purple, was sitting on Snowflake’s workstation. Her bracelet and earrings were eye-appealing, and her cutie mark of a diamond ring was declaring her status. She was frowning and checking students’ musical homework. The moment she finished one, she casually thrown the paper on the table.

Snowflake had never seen Mrs. Rich before, but her name and picture was on the first place of the donation list on the office wall.

The next thing Snowflake learned was that she might be a close relative of that Diamond Tiara sobbing beside her.

“Good afternoon, Mrs. Rich, how can I help you?”

Mrs. Rich sneered up to her, then continued looked at the papers in her hooves. She sighed and thrown all the papers, which made some of the papers slid to the gound.

Snowflake hesitated for a moment, then picked the fallen paper back on the table.

Ten seconds of silence.

“Why did you let my daughter to stand at the back of the room?” Mrs. Rich look up, straight towards Snowflake’s eyes, “Is she unworthy of having your class normally like other kids?”

“Well, you see…” Snowflake made up a smile, “She… fell asleep during the class, and um… You’ve seen that earlier too, Diamond Tiara didn’t do really well in her practices. I was worried about…”

“So this is your plan?” Mrs. Rich appeared a mocking look, “Humiliate her, and she’ll work day and night for your pity, like a maid?”

“I…”

“I fund to this school, yet normally pay the school fees every year, and this is the attitude you show me?”

“I didn’t…”

“Didn’t what? Those kids’ guardians had send me countless letters of complaint, and my mailbox could hardly had that size to store them ALL!”

Mrs. Rich slammed a pile of envelopes onto the table. The wind ruffled Snowflake’s hair. She opened her mouth, but her throat was stuck.

“I just can’t believe it,” Mrs. Rich slapped on her leg, “an outstanding student of East Westplain Associated University1, could struggle in teaching primary school students!”

Snowflake wanted to move away her hair strands blocking her sight, but her front legs were too stiff to bend.

“Enough. I don’t have much time to play with you.” Mrs. Rich stood from the chair, “I’ll attend your class next week. If nothing turned better, we will do what we should.”

“Yes.”

They left.

Snowflake was still standing at the same spot, hoping they didn’t see her tears refusing to drop.

Despite there was a little delay in her schedule, Snowflake trotted all the way and arrived in front of the door of Twilight’s dining room. She took a step aside, looked into the crystal wall, and fixed her hair, which had gotten messed up again during the travel. She closed her eyes, took a deep breath, and slowly breathed it out. “Nothing happened!” Snowflake practiced a smile.

Knock, knock.

“Come in!”

Snowflake saw the nightly dark blue princess at the first sight, because she sat on the side facing the door. Her crown and moon chest belt were the emblem of her royal power. She sat upright, too upright, with a penetrating gaze into the soul that only experienced royals could have.

“Snowflake! You came just in time!” Twilight’s voice relaxed Snowflake’s shoulder, she led Snowflake to the seat on the right side. “Luna, this is the music teacher in our town, Snowflake Mapleleaf. Snowflake, this is Princess Luna, princess of the night and dreams.”

“It’s my honor to meet your highness.” Snowflake bowed.

“Good evening. Your name is a perfect match to your appearance. I shall never forget.”

“So, Princess Luna,” Twilight started while slicing the bread, “what’s your plan of your southern tours?”

Luna recalled. “Well, I’ll pass the Ghastly Gorge, maybe a detour to Rock Farm. Then Appleloosa, Dodge City, Baltimare, Fillydelphia, then come back to Canterlot by train. Sorry I didn’t include Westplain District2 in it.”

“It’s okay. Westplain is too far anyway.” Snowflake gave an understanding smile.

“No matter how much book you read, it can’t be compared with seeing a real one.” Twilight poured the tea.

“Exactly.” Luna replied, “I once found that there was a lack of the war history in 1820s of Westplain. My sister told me despite our official declaration is that The Second Invasion of Changelings ended with their surrender in 1827, kirins in Westplain hadn’t driven all the invaders away until 1829.”

“Oh, that’s… really a heroic history I hadn’t discovered.” Twilight stopped drinking her soup.

“That’s not all,” Luna continued, “kirins are brave and battlewise, but their weapons were too old at that time. Over three million kirin soldiers sacrificed themselves to protect the southwest gate of Equestria. The overall population loss of kirins are still hard to estimate till today.”

Snowflake would never forget those. Her grandfather was a battlefield journalist. He told her the stories about how he managed to send the photos back to the rear area under changelings’ mopping-up operations. But she just couldn’t figure out why the conversation was led to such a heavy topic.

“On behalf of all other creatures in Equestria, we thank you for your service and sacrifice.”

Say something, Snowflake!

“So…” Twilight clapped her hooves, “If my memory serves me right, Luna you recently picked up Crystalian country tunes, right? I thought you two would have so much to talk about.”

“Yes. Music, as well as the culture, had changed a lot over a thousand of years.”

“Aww, don’t worry Luna, I’m sure you’ll get used to that.”

Two alicorns are still talking. Snowflake felt the round table was getting longer and longer, pushing her away from their voice. She looked into the silver plate, and saw a kirin with her dark-circled eyes, whose eyelids was getting heavier. Snowflake secretly fetched the fork off the table and pricked her leg. Suddenly the table was shortened, and she could hear the princesses talking again. But the effort was ineffective, a stronger wave of dizziness blurred her gaze.

“It’s kind of weird, actually,” Luna’s spoon was whirling in the bowl, “neither did I meet a kirin before, nor did I get into a kirin’s dream.”

A muffled sound.

Snowflake’s head crashed onto the table. The silver fork was flung up by her forehead, spun twice in the air, and clinkingly landed just less than an inch away from Luna.

“Is… that also a table manner of kirins?” The whirling spoon stopped.

Twilight bounced off the seat, hurriedly checked her out.

“She… fell asleep.”

  1. East Westplain Associated University(EWAU, 川东联合大学) is a university specified in this novel, located in eastern Westplain District.↩︎

  2. A location specified in this novel. Westplain District(西川特区) is a provincial-level administrative region in southwest Equestria, the main settlement area of kirins.↩︎
