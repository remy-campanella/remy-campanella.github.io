【RSM】 Chapter 2 Lots and Lots of Friends

发表于2026-04-11|更新于2026-04-11|艺术文学拙作Reminiscences de Snowflake Mapleleaf
|浏览量:

Snowflake had just found another feature of her room: her bed is near the window, which makes the morning light strong enough to wake her up, alarm clocks seemed to be useless here. She slept quite well, mostly because of the long journey. After the chat with Miss Cheerilee and Pinkie Pie last night, she learned that there were three music classes a week, one of them was just in this afternoon.

“Lots and lots of friends!” Pinkie’s words were still echoing in her head.

“Why’s she so confident about this?” Snowflake thought, while she finished washing up and went downstairs. She passed through a long corridor, with portraits of famous musicians.

Bathed in the dim light, the corridor seemed to tell the glorious history of the hall. This place had been used as a performance space since Ponyville was built, and was then expanded into a palatial hall about 50 years ago. During these time, musicians such as Liszt and Shostakovich gave widely known performances here, making it a cultural center of the region, even attractive to princesses.

Snowflake went out of the music hall and travelled along the street. She couldn’t help but to notice the busy stores and stands. Those houses seemed had never been developed through hundreds of years, for many of them are unexpectedly small and old-styled. However, not all houses stays the same, for there was an carousel-shaped fashion shop shining on the roadside.

The Carousel Boutique

Coutures of Rarity

“More like a ‘tent’ to me.” Snowflake thought. She knew nothing about clothing fashion. Because, you see, she didn’t normally wear clothes. The second reason is probably her better-than-nothing drawing technique. For a long period of time, Snowflake had concepted varies kinds of fine work of visual arts. But without drawing, all of them was unfortunately buried in ideas.

“Opal, get down from my unique and fabulous fabric now! I said, NOW!!!”

Looks like someone is going to experience the dark side of the talented designer. Snowflake sped her pace to avoid herself being captivated in the show. After several blocks of walk, she randomly entered a coffee shop.

“Welcome to Hay Café! May I take your order?” A waiter stallion, dressed in a neat white suit, welcomed Snowflake in a resounding yet polite voice.

“Umm…” Snowflake looked up and saw a long menu board of meals and snacks. Coffee, bread, cheese omlette, and… hay burgers? Isn’t hay supposed to build houses?

“I don’t know…” Snowflake was totally confused, not to mention thinking with an empty stomach. She could hardly read some of the words on the menu, what’s a “Shmoopy-doopy Sweetie-weety Apple Pie”?

“First time to be in Ponyville?”

Snowflake nodded.

“No wonder why you would stare at our menu for that long!” the waiter laughed and fetched a pointing stick.

“We do have a terribly long list of cuisines,” the waiter continued, “however we mainly serve for breakfast and afternoon tea.”

“I see.”

“Since this is your first meal here, I recommend you to choose one of our signature set meals: Ponyville Shimmers.”

“It’s just a cup of coffee and a fried egg sandwich.”

“Think about it again after you taste out our freshly picked mint.”

“Is that a…”

“Farmhouse Platter, a fruit dish.” The waiter pointed at a basket near the kitchen, “We only use fresh and excellent apples picked on the same day from the Sweet Apple Acres, providing you not only surprisingly fresh taste but also a boosted mood.”

“I think I’ll take a Ponyville Shimmers and the Shmoopy-doopy… What-so-ever Pie. Boy, that’s really an awkward name.”

“Our guests usually call it ‘Shmoopy Pie’ for short. All right, that’ll be 3 bits. Please have a seat and it’ll be ready in no time.”

Snowflake sat down by the table near the window. The summer sun rose up from the east hills, spraying the warming sparkle through the window on her face. It’s a pity to kill the morning time with such a pleasant weather. Speaking of which…

“Excuse me, I wonder where is the town library.”

“Well, we no longer reserve a library years ago,” the waiter put the dish on Snowflake’s table, “but we do have a princess of ‘egghead’.”

“E… egghead?”

“Princess Twilight Sparkle, or Princess of Friendship to be exact.” the waiter cleared the dirty dishes on another table onto his handcart. “Her castle is now serving as her living place and a library. Every time when the sun comes up, its crystal spire will reflect the sunshine to every corner of the town, you won’t miss it.”

“Thank you.” Snowflake found a light spot was glaring her eyes as he said.

Finding the way by staring at the crystal laser wasn’t a pleasant experience. Snowflake thought she would fall blind if she looked at that once more. But the castle wasn’t bad. The design used a tree-like structure at the bottom, and the upper castle was colored in blue and purple, letting Snowflake thought of the twilight at nightfall. However, Snowflake still didn’t feel like this is a formal castle. It had no fence, no walls, even no guards — unless the mailbox could fire letters at will.

Knock, knock.

No answer.

Knock, knock.

The door opened itself.

She tentatively let half of her body through the door, as if she is probing a trap room. But the door suddendly punched her to close and made her stumbled and fell to the crystal ground.

“Please don’t tell me this castle is enchanted.” Snowflake half-kneeled while checking her painful forehead, fortunately her horn is fine.

“Oh my Celestia! Are you okay?”

Showflake saw an… elf? No. He is small enough but covered with purple scales and green spikes. She also could feel his small claws and sharp nails while he was helping her up. All these appearances pointed to a species called…

“I’m Spike the dragon, princess Twilight’s No. 1 assistant. How can I help you?”

“I’m… ow… looking for Her Highness to fetch some books.”

“I’m afraid she’s not here. What kind of books?”

“Education and music?”

“So it’s you who is going to be the new music teacher of the town, Miss Mapleleaf, right?”

“How do you know?”

“Pinkie Pie told EVERYONE who entered Sugarcube Corner this morning.”

“She… did?”

“Yeah, you’re kind of a celebrity now.”

“Well, many thanks to her.” Snowflake followed Spike into the spiral staircase. Doors on the way all looked the same, Snowflake wonder how could Princess Twilight avoid entering the wrong room.

“There we are!” Spike opened a door and led Snowflake in.

Snowflake thought that waiter must cheated her. She look around while Spike climbed up the rolling ladder: it didn’t look like a public library at all! Half of the round room wall was horizontally separated by layers and layers of shelves, filled with books in various kinds of colors and sizes, as if the room were decorated by the rainbow of knowledge. In the center lied five nice and cozy bean bags, Snowflake could imagine herself lazily holding a romantic novel in those bags, relaxing from the hard work from all day. Perhaps the library focused on borrowing out instead of reading here?

“Education… education… music… music… Oh dear, I’m sure we have these kind of books, but Twilight may had moved the categories to the library of Friendship School. Now the books here are mainly about magic, you see.” Spike passed a book titled Assumptions of General Theory of Programming Magic.

“What a pity!” Snowflake passed back the book bigger than her face.

“Don’t worry, I could take you there.” Spike put the book back to the shelf and got down from the ladder.

“Really? I don’t want to bother you.”

“That’s my duty.” Little dragon said proudly.

Now the real fun begins. Friendship School’s library system was much more complete than that of Twilight’s castle. Instead of the empty central space, the library hall was full of ponies and other creatures, crossing back and forth through the steady bookshelves, like colorful streams running across stony riverbed. And, to Snowflake’s delight, the library was unexpectedly quiet in spite of having entered so much creatures. Outside the window you could see a lively lily pond, giving readers a seamless shift between work and leisure. Seems that the princess is not only an expert in magic but also a talented architect.

“Good morning Miss Mapleleaf, welcome to Ponyville.” Snowflake turned her sight and saw the speaker — an elegant alicorn, with night-like purple on her whole body, acting with stateliness but always revealing friendliness.

“Your Highness!” Snowflake bowed her head, but Twilight stopped her, “No need for that. I’m not that kind of princess, I prefer close friends rather than distant subjects.”

“We’re… friends?”

“It’s up to you. I don’t want the title of princess distracts me from making sincere friends.”

“Well I’m flattered.” Snowflake said, “I’m not good at making friends, not to mention with royals. I don’t know much about royal etiquette.”

“You don’t need to think of that with me.” Twilight smiled. “Actually, the other three princesses complaint much about that in private either. But you know, diplomacy…”

“I… see.” Snowflake replied, this was far beyond her knowledge. “And thank you for your books.”

“What are they?”

“The Acoustics, and The Weight of a Piano.” Snowflake showed her book title to Twilight.

“But you picked the original Crystalian edition, you could read Crystalian?” Princess’s eyes lit up.

Snowflake grinned while she was thumbing through the book content. “My grandfather used to study in the Crystal Empire for several years. I accidentally caught a glimpse of his notes when I was little. Then that interest continued.”

“Actually И Акустика1 is not my own book. It’s a donation from one of my students. My collection only takes up one fifth of the library.”

“No wonder why it’s so big!” Snowflake was marvelled as she looked at the top of the high shelves.

“If you love libraries, I could take you to the Grand Library of Canterlot and the Rosalov Library of the Crystal Empire.” Twilight replied. “By the way, the Crystal Empire is famous for its folk music.”

“I think I would plan long trips only after my job was stablized.” Snowflake shyly touched her hair. “But thanks anyway.”

“That’s okay, career is always the first.” An appreciative smile fled between Twilight’s lips. “Checking out the books at the front desk and you could bring them back to your home. Don’t forget to check the returning date!”

“You have my word.” Snowflake checked the titles of the two books again. “I have a lesson this afternoon. Sorry I have to leave now.”

“No problem.” Twilight replied, “If you need any help, you’re welcomed to ask. My door is always open.”

“That’s very generous of you, your highness. Goodbye!” Snowflake thanked.

“Bye!”

Twilight is still looking at the same direction.

“Gee, you had never been so welcoming in the first meets.” Spike returned beside Twilight, “I mean, you’ve got Rainbow Dash and Moondancer as book friends, but you seems to be especially interested in her.”

“She’s… quite different.” Twilight thought for a while. “But I’m definitely making her my book buddy.”

“Good afternoon, class.”

“Good~ afternoon~, Miss~ Mapleleaf~.”

Miss Snowflake Mapleleaf, the only kirin teacher in Ponyville, was now standing at the platform of the classroom of the Primary School of Ponyville. 25 pupils was sitting at their small desks, curiously observing the strange tutor in front of them.

“Maybe Pinkie Pie do finished some work for me in advance.” Snowflake was quite used to acquaintances never met. “But did Pinkie Pie tell you how to spell my name?”

“Uh… no.” Apple Bloom thought for some while and shook her head.

“Well, she missed that point.” Snowflake picked up a chalk. “My name is Snowflake Mapleleaf.” She turned around and wrote her name on the blackboard in a beautiful italic handwriting.

Snowflake Mapleleaf

“Snowflake, Miss Snowflake, Miss Mapleleaf, all fine.” Snowflake put down the chalk. She was happy that the class is running as planned. “I’m your new music teacher, from now on.”

“Sounds like you could beat Miss Cheerilee.”

“Good guess.” Not today, thank you. “May I have your name?”

“Diamond Tiara.” The pink filly with a diamond tiara answered a little disrespectfully.

“Well,” Snowflake asked the class again, “could anyone help me explain to her why I am here?”

The fillies looked at each other, the question was supposed to be come up by them.

“Why don’t we look at our textbooks?” Snowflake answered herself, “see whether Chancellor Neighsay’s preface would help.”

All ponies started carefully reading those words. Obviously, Miss Cheerilee’s Ponish class was effective, because two minutes later Scootaloo raised her hoof.

“I found one!” She said excitedly, “Start from this stage, students are now required to master basic music thoeries and techniques, including but not limited to advanced appreciation, multi-part chorus and basic com…, com…”

“Composition,” Snowflake added, “which means you’re going to write your own pieces. Don’t you think it’s a little bit unfair to push all these work to your dear Miss Cheerilee?”

“Yes!” The same answer burst out from 25 mouths. Miss Cheerilee giggled in the back of the classroom.

“Oh, no!” Diamond Tiara was disappointed, “Even music will be turned into boring essays.”

“You seems to have a point, Diamond Tiara.” Snowflake replied. “But I do not see music pieces as essays. Musical language and literal language are completely different concepts. When you hear a piece of music, the first impression is ‘how you feel’ rather than ‘what author say’. Good words always hide their thoughts behind various kinds of rhetorical devices, but music with direct and intense emotion is the most popular.”

Diamond Tiara need some time to digest these statements, her disrespect had quickly fled away as well.

“Um… Miss Snowflake, does that mean music classes won’t be fun anymore?” Pipsqueak asked with a little scare.

“I used to have the same question just like yours, Mister.” Snowflake thought for a while and said. “Music theory CAN be dull if the tutor only thinks about cramming principles into students’ heads. I’ve suffered from this when I was your age. For years I’ve been trying to come up with a teaching method that helps students embrace the true magic of music, and it’s my honor to have you with me. I’ll try to make this subject lively and fruitful, and don’t be afraid to tell me if something is hard to understand.”

“Oh, oh, can we do rock music?”

“Jazz!”

“Easy, class.” Snowflake reorganized the dicipline. “I’m sorry but the textbook consists of classical music, I’m afraid. However, we could treat them as an addition.”

“I just hope this class would be easier.” Sweetie Belle mumbled.

“I can’t tell,” Snowflake replied, “why don’t we find out in Chapter 1?”

  1. И Акустика(I Akustika), means “The Acoustics” in English.↩︎
