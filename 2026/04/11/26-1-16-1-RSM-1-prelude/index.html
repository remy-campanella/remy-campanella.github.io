【RSM】 Chapter 1 Prelude

发表于2026-04-11|更新于2026-04-11|艺术文学拙作Reminiscences de Snowflake Mapleleaf
|浏览量:

It was early in the morning. The first light of dawn crept over the horizon, spilling liquid gold through the dense canopy of the woods. The morning symphony began with a single, clear birdcall, a solitary note that hung in the cool air. It was soon answered by the chittering of squirrels and, finally, the boisterous antiphony of roosters, a sound the kirins had trusted for thousands of years to stir them from their slumber.

Snowflake was probably one of the first kirins to wake up. Sleep had never been forced to leave this early after she graduated from high school. She could still remember those days when she hurried herself up, stayed in a classroom for a whole day and back to sleep without any time to spare. Snowflake hated to be tardy, but she would never enjoy opening sleepy eyes earlier than 7:00.

“What a day!” she thought, “It’s half past six!”

A lazy yawn fled from her wide opened mouth. She rubbed her eyes and finally see who’s in the mirror: A kirin, undoubtly, with messy hair in color of passionate red of fire and a coat of soft, smooth white fur. She often felt a disconnection between her warm, inviting appearance and the quiet, reserved soul within. It was a mismatch that, she feared, sometimes drew the unexpected kind of attention.

Snowflake fetched a comb from the desk and started brushing. Yeah, that should be like herself in the photo hanging beside the window. The photo was taken by her mother in a sunny winter day when Snowflake was reading in a coffee shop. Crimson kept telling her how exquisite she looked when sunshine joined her being absorbed in her work. Moments like this are always rare to spare.

No time for recalling. Just like the old times, Snowflake went out her room and got downstairs, walked into the kitchen and brew herself a bowl of oat milk. Slightly heated it up, Snowflake held the bowl to the dining table and join in her early-prepared parents.

Wait…

“Mama? Papa? You’ve got up early too.” Snowflake was surprised, as Crimson and Westwind had already gotten washed, even Snowflake had got up an hour earlier than usual. They were sitting by the table, waiting for her to have breakfast.

“There was a loud bird noise outside our window.” Westwind lowered his newspaper and took a sip of tea, “Then I chatted with your mom in the bed until… Six?”

“A quarter past six, sleepyhead.” Crimson corrected, as she was wiping her transparent-framed glasses, “We were just too… overthinking to return to sleep. Ponyville, is it? A whole day on the train.”

“Mama, you take trains, I take trains, we even slept on a train before.” Snowflake knew that she was endlessly worrying again, “I’m not a child, Mom, I can take care of myself.”

“But Ponyville is so far away from here, you’re gonna stay all day on the train,” Crimson picked up Snowflake’s right hoof, “Is Ponyville safe? What if you get lost? Are there any kirins to help you?”

“I’ll be a music teacher, in a primary school, it’s not a big deal,” answered Snowflake, “Besides, you’ve met two Ponyville ponies months ago. They’re quite helpful, I’m sure they’re willing to help me as well.”

“Your mom may be overeacting, I guess, but she has a point.” Westwind’s eyes focusing on Snowflake’s, “Life isn’t as ideal as your poetic operas. You’re gonna consider realistic things: income, expenditure, social contact, and your job as a teacher itself isn’t an easy task.”

“I know, I know, just… Stop useless worrying right now.” Snowflake sat on the chair and started having breakfast. Just when she was drinking, something hit her: the taste of this oat milk still stays the same. But for how long? 22 years of her life living with her parents was going to end like a dream. Snowflake never wanted herself to live far away from home, but there weren’t much job opportunity for her to choose. Maybe her father is right, her future life wasn’t as ideal as she had dreamed. Her heart felt like walking in a misty valley.

“Stop worrying, huh? But I see one young lady had just wasted two minutes.” Crimson found Snowflake started daydreaming, with her spoon sinking in her bowl, staring at the ticking clock. Snowflake’s cheek flushed like a fillie caught stealing food from the fridge. She didn’t expect to show anything meaning she hadn’t prepared enough.

“You could always find my smallest mistake, don’t you mama?” Snowflake held the bowl up and drank up all milk, then returned to her room to fetch her bag. She didn’t have much things to take because her heavy luggage had been sent to Ponyville days ago. “It’s getting late, don’t forget the train!”, her father called.

It was definitely a sunny day, as the golden strings of sunshine warmly spread on Snowflake’s body, slightly lit her mood up. The family walked along the street and passed by the market, the library, and finally, the Kirin Station, which had just opened a new line to the Canterlot North Station not long ago. Snowflake found her parents weren’t the only two to farewell. There are two more waiting in front of the Kirin Station. As she moved near, she was quite sure — the shorter one was her friend, Autumn Blaze, and the taller one was her teacher, Rainshine.

“Oh, Madam, I… I hadn’t expected you would come.” Snowflake’s introvert nature taken up her mind again, as she quickly adjusting her mane and expression to look like more… respectful, you know.

“There weren’t many kirins got the chance to develop outside their hometown, since the new railway had just built.”, Rainshine replied, “I think this would be a memorable time for all of us. And I could tell Mr. Mapleleaf and Mrs. Morningcloud are quite proud of their daughter.”

“Her? Oh, no, no, Ms,” her father interjected, his voice just as flustered as Snowflake’s. “She still has so much to learn.”

“I don’t have any jobs far away, or parents willing to say goodbye, either.”, a jealous whisper came out from Autumn Blaze.

“Come on, Autumn, Ponyville will never hire a dumb to teach normal lessons if you were not there to cure me,” Snowflake replied. “Besides, I’m sure your parents will, they just haven’t got the chance.”

“Attention, passengers. Train H.695 to Canterlot North Station is now arriving at Kirin Station. All passengers boarding this train, please have your tickets ready for inspection at entrance A2. We wish you a pleasant journey. Thank you.”

“Oh my, is that late already?” Rainshine raised her head as she heard the broadcast, “It is time.”

“Thank you, thank you for being here, and accompany of all these years.” Snowflake was nearly in tears, “I just don’t know how to pay you back.”

Autumn put a hoof on Snowflake’s shoulder, “Then pay us back by contributing to the greatest career in the world — education.”

Snowflake kissed her parents and hugged the other two. After checking her saddle bag, she headed towards the security check.

“So long!” “Have a nice trip!” “Don’t forget to write!” Train stations has always been full of farewell words, but these five kirins didn’t say anything more. Snowflake got into her seat and pushed herself against the window to look outside. Through the busy crowd of kirins, she saw her family and friends were still there, watching at her direction, not knowing their sight would ever meet again.

“They treated me as a filly.” Snowflake thought, as she grinned and whispered, “But I’m glad they did that.”

It wasn’t an awful trip as she had considered. The carriage was relatively quiet so she had enough time and mood to think. She had heard about Ponyville when she was little, mostly from books of the libraries. Ponyville was described as a plain but fairytale place, known as the home of the Elements of Harmony. Despite of this, tourist guides still classified there as “countryside”. Weird enough, this also meant one of the princesses of Equestia could accept this title. “I had neither been to a city nor a town outside before,” Snowflake thought, “But my destiny has arranged me an extraordinary one.”

The train passed by tunnels and tunnels through the mountains, and then sped along the wide plain of central Equestria. The scenery and climate was completely different from that of the Kirin Village. Gone was the damp, clinging shadow of the mountains; instead, Snowflake was bathed in brilliant, unfiltered sunlight that stretched endlessly from one horizon to the other. The jagged, vertical view of the peaks were replaced by a rolling ocean of green velvet—a flat, fertile expanse where the wind raced freely without obstacle, rippling through the tall grain like waves in a golden sea. It was a landscape of openness and possibility, where the sky felt twice as large and the air carried the promise of a life unburdened by the heavy, silent stillness of home.

It was nearly at dusk when Snowflake finally arrived at Ponyville. The peaceful and neat town had passed its liveliest time of the day. The sun is sinking to the west, all you could feel was the atmosphere of leisure and relaxation. Ponyville ponies weren’t workaholic, they don’t believe working overtime would bring them true happiness. Instead, they tend to rise with the sun and rest with the sunset, which is similar to kirins.

“I just hope the post office is still open.” Snowflake thought, as she sped up the pace, her luggage should be delivered there by now.

“Hi Miss, how can I help you?” The mailmare Derpy Hooves gave a warm reception at the front desk.

“Oh, hello, I’m looking for a package delivered to Snowflake Mapleleaf. Is it here now?” Snowflake asked.

“Of course, let’s see…” Derpy lifted a piece of sheet paper so close to her eyes that the paper nearly poked into them. “Your package has arrived at … Kirin Village this morning.”

“What?! It can’t be!” Snowflake nearly shouted at her when she heard the message, fortunately she quickly calmed down. “Look, my home is far from here, undoubtedly. But that shouldn’t be the reason why my package hasn’t moved for a single mile.”

“Is it? Excuse me, let me check…” Derpy shook her head and gave a closer look to the list. “Oh, sorry! I made a mistake. Kirin Village is the sending place, not the destination. Your package has arrived our warehouse. Do you want me to fetch it for you?”

“No!” Snowflake almost had a stress reaction again. “I mean, why not we go to fetch it together, to make sure nothing goes wrong again.”

“Aye aye, captain!” The post mare blinked her unaligned eyes.

It took her a while to settle her accommodation, a room in the attic of Central Music Hall of Ponyville. It was previously used as a guest room for honoured musicians, but then fell out of use since there were better hotels. Snowflake thought the room satisfied her almost in every aspect: the basic furniture and facilities were old-fashioned but well maintained, separated by a wall to create a small, private sleeping area. To ensure the rest quality of the musicians, this room was also equipped with a magic barrier to block the noise. Through the window you can have a good view of the northern half of the town, as well as a glimpse of Canterlot.

“Whof! What a work!” Snowflake thought, “Hope I still have some time for Miss Cheerilee.”

The skilled and highly-praised teaching mare, Miss Cheerilee, was probably the only official teacher in Ponyville before the Friendship School was established. It may be hard to recognize her in the market crowds, but look into the playing ponies on the school playground, Miss Cheerilee is usually the leader.

For Snowflake, Miss Cheerilee was a colleague, a partner, more importantly, an experienced to help her be capable of the job.

So it’s a must for Snowflake to visit Cheerilee as soon as possible, a new employee always wants to leave a good impression on the senior.

At last, “any time you want”, said in Miss Cheerilee’s letter.

Miss Cheerilee’s house was just a few blocks away on the Center Street. Snowflake was preparing her lines in her heart as she trotting along the road. Her sight matched a pink pony’s coming out from a cake house. It’s funny for a pony to wear a color that eye-appealing, Snowflake thought. But the next second the pink pony appeared in her sight, she was right in front of Snowflake’s face.

“Um…, hello?” This was the most reliable greeting Snowflake could come up with.

“Ahhhh!” The pink pony made a large gasp, jumped nearly a floor high and dashed backed to the cake house, leaving Snowflake with great confusion. “Am I that scary?” She looked into the glass of the showcase as a mirror, everything is fine. She couldn’t react anything more but go on.

The light of Miss Cheerilee’s window was still on. Snowflake was delighted and tentatively knocked the door. Later, a purple-colored mare answered the door. “Good evening?”

“Good evening! My… name is Snowflake Mapleleaf, and I’m looking for Miss Cheerilee. Is she home?”

“I’m Cheerilee.” Miss Cheerilee replied, “Good evening, dear. I thought those postmares would have lost my letter in the long delivery. Come on in.”

“Please have a seat.”

Snowflake looked around. Miss Cheerilee’s home was yellow-toned, made you feel nice and cozy throughout the year. Beside the fire place there were three chairs surrounding a dining table, on which was a picture of her and eight kids. Snowflake carefully moved out a chair and sat beside the table.

“How’s the trip?” Miss Cheerilee returned with two cups of tea.

“Great, actually.” Snowflake took over the cups and placed them on the table. “Thank you. Just now I was greeted by a baker. I have to say she has a unique understanding of greeting new creatures.”

“A… baker?” Cheerilee asked and sat in the opposite direction.

“Isn’t she? A pink pony, with curly hair, smell like a frosting cake so I guess she works in a bakery?” Snowflake recalled.

“Oh, it’s her.” Cheerilee answerd. “Then I suppose you hadn’t got the gift for town’s new guest from her.”

“What’s that?” Snowflake asked.

Knock, knock.

“Why don’t you find out yourself?” Cheerilee smiled and pointed at the door.

Boom!

“Surprise!” Snowflake opened the door and was suddenly hit by a blast of confetti, then she saw a pink talking spring jumping up and down. “WELCOME TO PONYVILLE! Are you excited? I’m reallyreallyreally excited because you’re my new GUEST!!! I’ll take you to Twilight, Rainbow Dash, Apple Jack, Rarity, Fluttershy and you’ll have LOTS AND LOTS OF FRIENDS!”

“Uh, I…” Snowflake awkwardly cleaned up the last confetti on her face.

“All right, Pinkie Pie, I think she got it. This is Snowflake Mapleleaf, the new music teacher of our primary school.” Cheerilee came up and started introducing. “Snowflake, this is Pinkie Pie, the best baker in the town and the holder of Elements of Laughter.”

“It’s obvious, doesn’t it?” Snowflake reached her hoof. “Nice to meet you.”

“Nice to meet you too!” Pinkie grabbed her hoof and shook fiercely.

“Owhoawhoawhoawhoa K!”

“Why are we standing here?” Cheerilee asked. “Come on, let’s have some tea.”

“Wait, don’t go!” Pinkie called, as she grabbed a red cupcake out of her mane, “Hope you have a successful interview!”

“Pinkie, her interview has passed.” Cheerilee explained with a smile. “You could join us if you want.”

“YEAH! PARTY!”

“No, no, Pinkie, just a casual meet, with chat and tea.”

“Oh, um, I’d love to, but… I’m really not good at… loud noises. I get overwhelmed easily.” Snowflake followed.

“Aww—, don’t be shy,” Pinkie blinked her eyes, “even Fluttershy would enjoy one, that shouldn’t be a problem.”

“Let’s go,” Cheerilee called, “the tea is getting cold.”
