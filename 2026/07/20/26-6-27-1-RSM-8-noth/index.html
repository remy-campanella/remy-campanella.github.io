【RSM】 Chapter 8 Nothing Happened

发表于2026-07-19|更新于2026-07-19|艺术文学拙作Reminiscences de Snowflake Mapleleaf
|浏览量:

Snowflake awoke.

Her eyelids flickered open, then shut, and parted again as the morning light pressed through the window. She slowly turned her body and faced up, what showed in sight was not the crystal dome, but blurrily the brown oblique ceiling instead.

She laid still for a moment, let the fact settle: she was in her bed, not mud, not water.

But the next thing she felt was wetness. Her bed sheet, herself, her blanket were all soaked in sweat. Peeling her body off the blanket let some cold air in, the coldness ran through her back, which let her trembled. She lifted herself up and sat on the bed, with some hair sticked on her face and neck. The was an unpleasant smell in the air.

AH-CHOO!

The sneezing sound was all of a sudden, the movement sent a jolt through her already-dizzy head, and led to a intermittent headache with her heart beat. She couldn’t help but moaned, the voice was dry and weak. Her sound was still fine.

She moved aside her tangled hair strands and looked at the clock on her desk. She was supposed to get up by 8:00 on days off, but somehow she had slept until 9. What did it mean to wake up by 9? At least nothing today. The second hand spun over and over, she fell into a daze until the minute hand pointed at “6”.

What happened last night?

She walked into a… river, yes. and a pair of tiny little hooves grabbed her back.

Not quite like a dream.

She looked down on her hooves, at least not with dirt. SHe turned the right one and saw the scar, which she thought there is no pain anymore.

She slowly moved off the bed. Her hooves felt like they were pressing into cotton, hard to find a point to stand. She went to wash. She opened the tap too wide, and water splashed across the counter, she didn’t wipe it. The kirin in the mirror had lighter dark circles around her eyes, but she looked much tired than before. She tried to comb her mane, but stopped halfway. Too lazy to keep on.

When she passed through the corridor, the light was dark. Those pegasus pushed the clouds very low, stuffed the whole sky, not letting out any morning light. The corridor was even darker, the portraits on the wall had been reduced to outlines. One of them was Franz Liszt, Snowflake would pass under it everyday, but seldom stopped to watch, not today either. She just kept walking, following a approximately straight path in the memory her body had kept, until her front left hoof touched the wall foot.

She froze for a moment, and found she had deviated by at least half her body width. How did she get here? What was she even thinking? She searched her mind, nothing there. Her consciousness had stuck the moment she stepped into the corridor, like a sentence written by a pen suddenly broke, the later half unintentionally left blank. She turned and kept walking. If she wasn’t on the path she walked everyday, she would have walked into the wall.

She went out of the concert hall and got on the street.

The light on the street wasn’t bright either, but at least there was more space. She walked very slow, as ponies and carriges surpassed her quickly on by one. She walked by a bakery, a spa, and an old oak tree crooked beside the road. She saw them, but they just passed in front of her eyes, remaining nothing. Her eyes felt like covered with frosted glass, her ears felt stuffed with cotton. She saw and heared those objects, but nothing stayed.

Snowflake didn’t know how long she had walked, until she raised her head and saw the sign above the gates in front of her.

Primary School of Ponyville

Today is Saturday, the day off.

Those characters pierced her daze like needles, she trembled like electric-shocked, Mrs. Rich’s words, voice, tone, all floated up from the bottom of her memory. And the last sentence Mrs. Rich threw to her crammed those into her chest, her breath suddenly became thinner, her heartbeat forgot a beat.

The class.

Snowflake turned around. She didn’t have any cue of the next class. If she didn’t have one, then she’ll go to find one. She won’t be picky of new ideas, but whatever choice she made, she just can’t stand there.

She saw the spark shape of the crystal castle.

Behind which was a building with long windows, the library of Friendship School.

The first clear command her brain had ever made was: go there.

She executed, she made a step.

“Useful?”

A voice echoed in her mind, casually and disdainfully, Snowflake had to convince it before she went on. Yesterday, she worked, but got yelled. It’s indeed not useful to go there, at least for now.

Snowflake turned back.

“Done?”

Another question hit her on her forehead. She stopped immediately, her hooves stomped on the ground, the jolt sent a dull ache through her legs.

What on earth is happening? Never mind. I really should go prepare for…

“Can you make it?”

It’s true. Whatever she did, she could only perform a same class like before. Not better than not going to…

“Leave without making it?”

Snowflake thrown herself on the ground, with her body leaning against the streetlamp. Her eyes looked at the ponies and carraiges travelling along the road. They all had directions, they’re all moving, except her stopped here.

Behind Snowflake was a flower shop. Roseluck was cutting the twigs of those plants. She raised her sight, then continue her cutting. It’s a custom order for a client, there should be exactly 12 blossoms, uniformly blooming on the limbs. Better not ruin it.

Snowflake didn’t go to the library at last, she went to the park, walking on the bank of Saddle River. The grassland turned yellow, lying on the ground toward the same direction. She chose a bench, then lied on it.

There were some rustling in the tree above her face. Two swallows, more like a couple, shot out from the canopy, circled in the air for two rounds, then flew south. Their body moved fast in the grey sky, quickly shrank into to small dots, then disappeared in the forest farther.

Snowflake looked towards there, knowing that those swallows would cross the mountain ranges of the northern Westplain through valleys, to the warmer south. Two months later—maybe longer, considering the climate—they’ll arrive at Kirin Hills1, and enter Guangdu Plain2, finding their old homes scattered around villages and towns. The red maple leaves will cover each road in Kirin City, no matter wide or narrow. The air will be filled with the sweetness of the osmanthus and a touch of dry and cool autumn.

Every evening, every day, this time of every year, Snowie would sit in front of the window, waiting for the first flock of swallows stopped by her house. One day, two swallows, probably a male and a female, started building a nest on a branch exactly outside her window, stacking mud and sticks into a hemispherical bowl. Snowie found it, she nearly shouted out, but hurriedly muffled her mouth, in case of not scaring them. She fetched the camera and took a lot of photos. She even banned her parents from talking loudly in her room—the swallows were outside, they might get frightened.

That nest stood there for a week.

One night, Kirin City was caught in a strong windy weather. By the next day she trotted to the window, the branch was empty, only remaining some mud stamps and a broken stick. The swallows never came back, as if they have never stayed there.

Long period after that, every day, that time of every year, Snowie would look up to the sky, and said to herself: if she have a pair of wings, she could catch up those swallows. She wanted to tell them, don’t go, please stay. After they left, the night was just too quite to fell asleep.

She kept waiting. She didn’t grow a pair of wings. And that wish was never mentioned since she can’t remember when.

Snowflake was still lying on the bench, looking towards the direction swallows had gone, for a long time. River flows near from her, the running sound was constant and stable, like a living promise. She closed her eyes for a little while, and by the time she opened them again, the sky is getting dark. The streetlamps had just been lit, glowing halos around each pole.

She stood up, walking along the path she had come, but had never left the route.

Snowflake opened the concert hall door, stepped on the stairs and returned to the attic. The room was the same as moring, the bed was not cleaned. Her stomach started to growl, and it took some time for her to realise what does it mean.

She hadn’t eaten for the whole day.

She opened the cupboard, there was only a loaf of bread inside. She pulled it out, bit on one end, and tore at it like a timberwolf. The bread was resilient, and she hadn’t torn it apart until her facial muscle felt sore. Some crumbs fell on her chest and beside her hooves. She didn’t care about them at first, but chew that bite up, then another bite, then another one. She felt something was sqeezing in her throat, she couldn’t remember whether she had chewed it enough, then fetched the cup and poured herself some water, then drank and swallow the bread all down.

She leaned against the cupboard and caught the breath, then bent down, collecting the crumbs from the floor, and threw them into trash. The light cast her shadow on the white wall, the movements were slow yet orded.

She refolded the wrapping paper and put it back to the cupboard. Then she sat on her bed, looking the dark blue sky outside the window, a thought surfaced, sticky and inescapable.

You did nothing today.

The day was wasted. You still got a class to prepare, and Mrs. Rich would watch, there were so many things waiting for you, and during the time you’ll gonna lose your job, you simply lied in the park for a whole day.

She waited for the pain the thought would bought, as usual. But the pain was lighter that she had expected, felt like she had become immune to that.

And also another thing, also there—

She couldn’t name it for now.

She felt she wasn’t that Snowflake before. She didn’t know who she was. But she found she wasn’t that afraid any more.

The light went off. She kept sitting there, in the darkness, the day ended like nothing happend.

  1. Kirin Hills(麒麟丘陵), where Kirin City locates.↩︎

  2. Guangdu Plain(广都平原), where Guangdu City locates. Guangdu City, capital of Westplain District.↩︎
